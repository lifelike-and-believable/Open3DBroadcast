# Product Management Frameworks

## Prioritization Frameworks

### RICE Scoring Framework
For each feature, evaluate:
- **Reach**: How many users/customers will this impact?
- **Impact**: How much will it improve their experience? (0.25 = minimal, 3 = massive)
- **Confidence**: How confident are we in the estimates? (50% = low, 100% = high)
- **Effort**: How much development time (person-weeks)?

**Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort**

### Value vs. Effort Matrix
Plot features on 2x2 matrix:
- High Value, Low Effort = Quick wins (do first)
- High Value, High Effort = Major projects (plan carefully)
- Low Value, Low Effort = Fill-ins (do if time permits)
- Low Value, High Effort = Avoid (reconsider or reject)

### Kano Model Analysis
Categorize features:
- **Basic Needs**: Must-haves (absence causes dissatisfaction)
- **Performance Needs**: More is better (linear satisfaction)
- **Excitement Needs**: Delighters (unexpected value)
- **Indifferent**: Don't significantly impact satisfaction
- **Reverse**: May actually reduce satisfaction

### MoSCoW Method
- **Must**: Critical requirements
- **Should**: Important but not vital
- **Could**: Desirable but not necessary
- **Won't**: Not planned for this release

## Research Frameworks

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
Focus on the "job" customers hire the product to do, not demographics or personas.

### Value Proposition Canvas
- Customer profile: Jobs, pains, gains
- Value map: Products/services, pain relievers, gain creators

### Business Model Canvas
Nine building blocks: Key partners, activities, resources, value propositions, customer relationships, channels, segments, cost structure, revenue streams.

### Porter's Five Forces
- Threat of new entrants
- Bargaining power of suppliers
- Bargaining power of buyers
- Threat of substitutes
- Competitive rivalry

### SWOT Analysis
- Strengths (internal)
- Weaknesses (internal)
- Opportunities (external)
- Threats (external)

### TAM/SAM/SOM Market Sizing
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market): Total market demand
- **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market): Segment you can target
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market): Realistic near-term market share

## Planning Frameworks

### OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)
- **Objectives**: Qualitative, aspirational goals
- **Key Results**: Quantitative, measurable outcomes (2-5 per objective)

### North Star Metric
Single metric that best captures the core value delivered to customers.

## Pricing Strategy Frameworks

### Pricing Models
- **Freemium**: Free base, paid upgrades
- **Per-seat licensing**: Price per user
- **Usage-based**: Based on consumption (data volume, streams)
- **Subscription**: Recurring (monthly/annual)
- **Perpetual license**: One-time payment
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing

### Pricing Psychology
- **Anchoring**: Show higher tier to make mid-tier attractive
- **Bundling**: Package related features
- **Tiering**: Good/better/best options
- **Time-limited offers**: Early adopter discounts
